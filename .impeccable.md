## Design Context

### Users
This product is an operator-facing fintech admin portal used by internal teams to review users, trading accounts, IB relationships, commissions, finance records, support tickets, referrals, campaigns, and system configuration. Users work in a dense, decision-heavy environment where they need to scan quickly, compare states, and move confidently between related modules without losing context. The interface should optimize for operational clarity over decoration and treat list, drawer, and detail views as part of one connected workflow.

### Brand Personality
Calm, trustworthy, precise.

The product should feel like a serious financial operations system rather than a marketing site or a generic SaaS template. It should communicate control, stability, and maturity. Visual confidence should come from hierarchy, spacing, and consistency, not from loud colors or excessive effects.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a premium admin style that is minimalist and restrained, but still polished and high-end. Favor compact but breathable spacing, soft layered surfaces, measured contrast, and clear section rhythm over flashy gradients or aggressive borders. The product should feel elegant through precision and refinement, not through visual noise. Dark mode is the stronger primary mood, but light mode must feel equally intentional and fully readable. The visual language should stay minimal, system-first, and modular across all modules.

Reference cues to borrow from, without copying directly:
- Linear: restraint, density control, and clean interaction states
- Stripe Dashboard: operational clarity, hierarchy, and premium infrastructure feel
- Vercel: refined spacing, calm typography, and minimal-but-finished surfaces

Avoid:
- CRM-like profile-heavy layouts
- consumer-product whimsy
- over-decorated glassmorphism
- harsh border-heavy panels
- dashboard-only visual special cases

### Design Principles
1. Make information hierarchy obvious before adding visual treatment.
2. Keep relationship, operational state, and traceability clearer than decoration.
3. Use one shared shell and one shared component language across all admin modules.
4. Prefer compact, readable, scan-first layouts over full-width sprawl.
5. Keep interaction feedback subtle, calm, and consistent.
6. Aim for minimalist restraint first, then add precision and polish rather than visual flourish.
